How To Join Two Surahs One may choose from any of the three methods to join the surahs they’re reading from the Qur’aan: Method 1: Joining the end of the first Surah, with Al-Basmalah and with the beginning of the next Surah (Joining everything together) Example: وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌۢ بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمِ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقْ Method 2: Pausing between the end of the first Surah, Al Basmalah and the beginning of the next Surah (Pausing between all three) Example: وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ كُفُوًا أَحَدْ بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمْ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقْ Method 3: Pausing between the end of the first Surah and joining Al Basmalah with the beginning of the next Surah Example: وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ كُفُوًا أَحَدْ بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمِ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقْ Note: It is not permissible to do the following method: Joining the end of the first Surah, followed by Al-Basmalah and pause before starting the next Surah Example: وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُۥ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌۢ بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمْ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَق One should not recite Al-Isti’aathah between two surahs, however Al-Basmalah is permissible to recite between two surahs except between Surat Al Anfaal and At-Tawbah (one would only recite Al-Isti’aathah between Surat Al Anfaal and At-Tawbah
