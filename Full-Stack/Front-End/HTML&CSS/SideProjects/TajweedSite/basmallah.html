الْبَسْمَلَةُ Al-Basmalah Meaning: بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمِ (In the name of Allah, The Most Merciful, The Most Beneficent) The only surah to include Al-Basmalah as a verse is Surat An-Naml. Whoever wants to recite from the Qur’aan must recite Al-Basmalah except Surat At-Taubah, it is permissible to recite Al-Basmalah when reciting from the middle of any surah including Surat At-Taubah. Before reciting from the Qur’aan, one must first recite 1) Al-Isti’aathah, followed by 2) Al-Basmalah and 3) the surah they wish to read One may choose from any of the four methods to recite from the Qur’aan: Method 1: Joining Al-Isti’aathah with Al-Basmalah and the Surah Example: أَعُوْذُ بِاللهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمِ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقِ Method 2: Pausing between Al-Isti’aathah, Al Basmalah and the Surah Example: أَعُوْذُ بِاللهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمْ بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمْ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقِ Method 3: Reciting Al-Isti’aathah, pausing and joining Al-Basmalah with the Surah Example: أَعُوْذُ بِاللهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمْ بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمِ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقِ Method 4: Joining Al-Isti’aathah with Al-Basmalah, pausing and then starting the Surah Example: أَعُوْذُ بِاللهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمنِ الرَّحِيمْ قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ ٱلْفَلَقِ
