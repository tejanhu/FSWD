The Four Situations When the Laam is heavy in the name of Allah Laam will always be pronounced light, except in the case of the name of Allah where the following conditions apply: Laam will be pronounced heavy if it comes after a fatha i.e: قَالَ اللهُ, مَا شَاءَ اللهُ Laam will be pronounced heavy if it comes after a dhammah i.e: عَبْدُ اللهِ, فَزَادَهُمُ اللهُ Laam will be pronounced heavy if it comes after a “saakin” non-voweled letter that is immediately after a letter with fatha i.e: عَلَى اللهِ , إِلَى اللهِ Laam will be pronounced heavy if it comes after a “saakin” non-voweled letter that is immediately after a letter with dhammah i.e: اِتَّقُوْا اللهَ, قَالُوْا اللَّهُمَّ The Three Situations When the Laam is light in the name of Allah Laam will be pronounced light if it comes after a kasrah Example: بِاللهِ , قُلِ اللّهمَّ , فِي دِيْنِ اللهِ Laam will be pronounced light if it comes after a “saakin” non-voweled letter that is immediately after a letter with kasrah Example: وَيُنَجِّي آللهُ Laam will be pronounced light if it comes after a letter with tanween Example: قَوْمًا آللهُ مُهْلِكُمْ
