Al-Qalqalah (Echoing Sound) Al-Qalqalah is the bouncing/echoing sound that occurs, when any of the following 5 letters: (ق ط ب ج د) is found with a sukoon, which can be remembered easily via the phrase: قُطْبُ جَدْ There are two types of Al-Qalqalah: Major: (The Qalqalah will be more heavier) When stopping on a Qalqalah letter with shaddah at the end of a word i.e: الحَقّ، الحَجّ Minor:(The Qalqalah will be more lighter) When reaching a qalqalah letter that is “saakin” non-voweled in the middle of a word i.e:أَجْرًا، تَطْمَئِنُّ، إِبْرَاهِيْمُ، مُحِيطْ، مُنِيبْ Note: Letters of Qalqalah always are “saakin” non-voweled and give a sound similar to the vowel coming before it in all situations, however it is NOT permissible for the qalqalah to change into a fatha, dhammah or kasrah.
