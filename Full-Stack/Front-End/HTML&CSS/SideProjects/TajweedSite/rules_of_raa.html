Seven Situations When Raa will be Heavy Raa can sometimes be pronounced light and sometimes heavy, this all depends on its conditions. Note: One must pay close attention to the following eight heavy conditions that apply to the letter Raa. Raa will be pronounced with Tafkheem (Heaviness) if it has a fatha i.e: (رَمَضَان , رَحْمَةِ رَبِّكَ) Raa will be pronounced with Tafkheem (Heaviness) if it has a dhammah i.e: (كَفَرُوْا , تَبَرُّوْا وَتَتَّقُوْا , رُوْحُ القُدُسِ , رُحَمَاءُ) 3) Raa will be pronounced with Tafkheem (Heaviness) if it has a sukoon and the letter befores has a fatha i.e: (أَرْسَلْنَا , أَرْبَعِيْنَ , مَرْجِعُكُمْ) 4) Raa will be pronounced with Tafkheem (Heaviness) if it has a sukoon and the letter befores has a dhammah i.e: (المُرْسَلُوْنَ , وَقُرْءَانٍ , بِقُرْبَانٍ) 5) Raa will be pronounced with Tafkheem (Heaviness) if it is a “saakin” non-voweled letter and a hamzatul wasl comes immediately before it i.e: اِرْجِعِي إِلَى رَبِّكِ , ارْجِعُوْا , ارْكَعُوْا , اِرْكَبُوْا, إِنِ ارْتَبْتُمْ أَمِ ارْتَابُوْا , لِمَنِ ارْتَضَى 6) Raa will be pronounced with Tafkheem (Heaviness) if it is a “saakin” non-voweled letter and the letter after raa is an elevation letter that has not got a kasrah and the letter before raa has an original kasrah in the same word i.e: (إِرْصَادًا , مِرْصَادًا , فِرْقَة , قِرْطَاس) 7) Raa will be pronounced with Tafkheem (Heaviness) when it is a “saakin” letter due to “waqf” stopping and the letter coming before it is also a “saakin” letter and immediately before it is a fatha or dhammah i.e: (الفَجْرْ, العَصْرْ, الوَتْرْ, الصَّخْرْ, خُسْرْ) Five Situations when Raa will be light Note: One must pay close attention to the following 5 light conditions that apply to the letter Raa. Raa will be pronounced with Tarqeeq (Lightness) if it has a kasrah i.e.: (رِجَالٌ , فَرِحَ المُخَلَّفُوْنَ) Raa will be pronounced with Tarqeeq (Lightness) if it comes after a kasrah i.e: (فِرْعَوْنَ , وَاصْبِرْ , لَشِرْذِمَةٌ) Raa will be pronounced with Tarqeeq (Lightness) if it becomes “saakin” due to “waqf” and before it came a yaa saakinah i.e.: (خَطِيْرْ, كَبِيْرْ, خَبِيْرْ) Raa will be pronounced with Tarqeeq (Lightness) if it is a “saakin” non-voweled letter and the letter before raa is an elevation letter that has not got a (dhammah or fatha) i.e.: (مِصْرْ , قِطْرْ) Raa will be pronounced with Tarqeeq (Lightness) when it comes before a small Alif in a word where the fatha is in between a fatha and kasrah and the alif in between an alif and a yaa and this occurs only once in the Qur’aan in verse 41 of Surat Hud i.e:
