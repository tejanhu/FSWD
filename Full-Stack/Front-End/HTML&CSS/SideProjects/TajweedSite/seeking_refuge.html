الْإِسْتِعَاذَةُ Al-Isti’aathah Meaning: أَعُوْذُ بِاللٌّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ (I seek refuge in Allah from Shaitaan the accursed) Whoever wants to recite from the Qur’aan must recite Al-Isti’aathah because Allah commanded us in the Qur’aan in Surat An-Nahl verse 98: {فَإِذَا قَرَأْتَ الْقُرْآنَ فَاسْتَعِذْ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ} “When you recite the Qur’aan, seek refuge with Allah from Shaitaan, the accursed” There are three places where one may say it quietly to oneself When you recite the Qur’aan (outside the prayer) When you recite the Qur’aan while in the prayer When you recite the Qur’aan within a group of people who are learning or reciting the Qur’aan and you are not the first to read There are two places where one may say it loudly When you’re the first to recite the Qur’aan and someone is listening to you When you recite the Qur’aan within a group of people who are learning or reciting the Qur’aan and you are the first to read
